What is Student Robotics?

Student Robotics is an exciting annual competition that challenges teams of 16–18 year-olds to build fully autonomous robots. Participating teams must design, build and test their robots, ready to compete against other teams.

The organisation started out as an idea from a few students at the University of Southampton and since then, it has grown massively and continues to atttract students and schools from across the UK and Germany.

Last year, we ran our competition at Newbury Racecourse and it was a great success! We had over fifty teams taking part and a large number of volunteers.

All of what we achieve as an organsation is lead by our team of hard working volunteers. The most important role as an volunteer is to make sure the teams have a robot to compete with each year and get them excited about robotics and engineering. As a volunteer, you will be assigned a school and visit them as often as possible (usually on a weekly basis) to talk with the team and make sure everything is going to plan. As well as mentoring competitors, we also have volunteers who get involved in the organisation of Student Robotics: everything from writing software, designing hardware, talking to sponsors and much more is open to you

Your first mentoring

The idea of mentoring can be a little scary at first, but we aim to make you as comfortable as possible in your new role.

I expected it to be more like teaching, but it was more about supporting the teams, I think that’s a good idea because it doesn’t waste their time. — A new volunteer after first time mentoring

For your first few mentoring sessions, we always make sure that new volunteers are sent out with an experienced mentor, known as a tutor. The idea behind this is to let you watch how your tutor deals with situations and also let you ask them questions about mentoring in general. Your tutor will also help you to communicate with the school to agree meeting times and other details.

Glossary

Volunteer
Someone just like you! A volunteer (often known as a blueshirt based on the colour of our t-shirts) is someone who helps ensure every we year we run a fantastic competition. The job of a volunteer is to primarily to make sure that teams have an enjoyable and useful experience doing Studen Robotics, but there are plenty of opportunities outside that including writing software, designing hardware and liasing with sponsors and schools.
Trac
Trac is an external project management system that we use to manage all aspects of the organisation. Tickets track things which need to be done.
Gerrit
Gerrit is tool that helps lots of people collaborating on the same piece of code to review and make comment. Anyone with an account can submit patches (sets of changes) against our software via Gerrit.
Mailing List
Our mailing list is a way of communicating with a large number of people at once by sending an email and letting it be received by many others.
